Research

As a cosmologist, I am fascinated by the history of the Universe on the large scales. My main research interest is to understand how much we can learn about the cosmic history and the underlying theory of gravity using gravitational wave observations, as well as its combinations with large-scale structures and other cosmological probes.

I am currently a member of the Brazilian Unit of the third generation detector Einstein Telescope and have been working at ICTP-SAIFR in the group of Prof. Riccardo Sturani. By the time of third generation detectors we will accumulate maybe hundreds of thousands of gravitational wave events called 'standard sirens', some small fraction of which will be accompained by electromagnetic counterparts. This is a very exciting scenario for measuring the current expansion rate of the Universe, measuring properties of the dark energy and testing our standard cosmological model, large goals for which my research has been contributing.

Some of my current research topics are:

  • Model-independent tests of the dark energy with standard sirens
  • Cross-correlations of dark standard sirens and the large-scale structure
  • Gravitational wave parameter estimation
